Allama Muhammad Iqbal

The Poet of the East

Allama Iqbal was a visionary philosopher, poet, and political thinker whose ideas laid the foundation for the creation of Pakistan. His life was a journey of deep thought, powerful expression, and unwavering dedication to the revival of the Muslim world.

Birth and Family

Allama Muhammad Iqbal was born on Friday, November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, Punjab (now Pakistan) at around 5:30 a.m. His father, Sheikh Noor Muhammad, was a tailor and a spiritual man. His mother, Imam Bibi, was kind-hearted and wise. Iqbal had siblings, including a younger brother named Atta Muhammad.

Early Education

He studied at Scotch Mission College in Sialkot where he learned Persian and Arabic. Later, he moved to Government College Lahore where he excelled in philosophy and literature.

Education Abroad

In 1905, Iqbal went to England to study law at the University of Cambridge and joined Lincoln’s Inn. He later earned a PhD in Philosophy from University of Munich, Germany. His thesis was titled "The Development of Metaphysics in Persia."

Marriage and Children

Allama Iqbal married three times. His first wife was Karim Bibi. He had children including Javed Iqbal, who became a judge and a scholar.

Poetry and Philosophy

Iqbal’s interest in poetry began early. He was influenced by Rumi and other Persian poets. He wrote in both Urdu and Persian about self-respect, spirituality, and unity of the Muslim world.

Travels and Influence

Iqbal visited England, Germany, Italy, and Egypt, where he expanded his political and philosophical vision. His thought helped shape the idea of a separate Muslim homeland: Pakistan.

Death

Allama Iqbal passed away on Sunday, April 21, 1938 at around 4:00 a.m. in Lahore. He is buried near Badshahi Mosque. His tomb remains a symbol of respect and pride.

Allama Muhammad Iqbal

The Poet of the East

Allama Iqbal was a visionary philosopher, poet, and political thinker whose ideas laid the foundation for the creation of Pakistan. His life was a journey of deep thought, powerful expression, and unwavering dedication to the revival of the Muslim world.

Early Life & Background

Allama Iqbal was born on Friday, November 9, 1877, in Sialkot, Punjab (now in Pakistan). His birth time is not officially recorded.

His father, Sheikh Noor Muhammad, was a tailor and a religious man. His mother, Imam Bibi, was a kind and humble woman.

Iqbal had five siblings. He was the second youngest in the family.

He started his early education in a local mosque where he learned Arabic and the Quran. Later, he joined the Scotch Mission School in Sialkot.

In 1895, he married Karim Bibi, but the marriage did not last long. He later had two more marriages.

Education & Institutions

Iqbal completed his matriculation from Scotch Mission School in Sialkot in 1893.

He went to Murray College, Sialkot, where he studied philosophy and literature.

In 1895, he joined Government College, Lahore, and earned his Bachelor’s in Philosophy in 1897 and Master’s in Philosophy in 1899 with top honors.

In 1905, he traveled to England and studied at Trinity College, University of Cambridge, where he earned a second Bachelor’s in Arts.

Later, he enrolled at the University of Munich, Germany, and completed his Ph.D. in Philosophy in 1908.

Travels & Poetry Era

Allama Iqbal developed a love for poetry during his student years in Lahore. His teacher, Professor Arnold, encouraged him to express deep ideas through verse.

He wrote in Persian and Urdu, focusing on self-awareness, unity, and spiritual awakening.

Iqbal visited several countries during his academic and political journey, including England, Germany, Italy, Egypt, Afghanistan, and Iran. These travels helped shape his ideas about the East and West.

His poetry started gaining recognition after his famous collection “Bang-e-Dra” (The Call of the Marching Bell) in 1924. His verses inspired millions in the Muslim world.

Family, Death & Legacy

Allama Iqbal had three children: Aftab Iqbal, Muneera Bano, and Javed Iqbal, who later became a respected judge.

He passed away on Sunday, April 21, 1938, in Lahore at the age of 60. He died at dawn and was buried near the Badshahi Mosque.

Today, he is known as Mufakkir-e-Pakistan (The Thinker of Pakistan) and Shair-e-Mashriq (Poet of the East). His thoughts greatly influenced the creation of Pakistan.
